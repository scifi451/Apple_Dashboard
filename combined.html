If you turn off the iPhone segment it is much easier to see how much the iPhone category just dwarfs the other parts of Apple's company on the graph. With the iPhone category turned off it is also much easier to see how much faster the Services sector has been growing compared to the other categories that are still visible.

I touched on in it the Services page, it is an important thing to note is that the Services revenue is more consistent since it is ongoing revenue as compared to the hardware categories of the Mac, iPad, iPhone, and Wearables which are much more seasonal and spike during the holiday quarter which is fiscal quarter one for Apple.

While the iPhone is the main driver of revenue for the company when you add up the other categories you can tell why Apple ended up at #3 overall in the Fortune 500 rankings.
